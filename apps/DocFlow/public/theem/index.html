Essay · 1,124 words · Pathway 02

Intelligence needs friction.

The most useful AI may not be the one that answers first, but the one that makes our decisions easier to see.

Every new writing tool promises to remove friction. A cleaner canvas. A faster completion. A sentence before you have fully decided what you mean. Speed feels like progress because it is easy to measure.

But writing is not only the production of language. It is the gradual exposure of a position: the claims we are willing to defend, the evidence we trust, the audience we imagine, and the trade-offs we accept.

When the interface hides those decisions, fluency can arrive before authorship.

The better interaction is not another chat box. It is a surface where intention has shape—where a writer can compare several possible structures, pull the tone toward urgency or restraint, lock a paragraph that already feels true, and see what must change when one premise moves.

The goal is not to slow writers down. It is to put friction in the right place: before commitment, around consequential choices, and nowhere else. The machine can still move quickly. The writer should remain able to see the path.
